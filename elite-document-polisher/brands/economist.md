# The Economist Brand Guide

## Overview
The Economist is the world's leading source for analysis of international business and world affairs. Their visual identity conveys intellectual authority, editorial excellence, and timeless sophistication.

## Visual Identity

### Typography
- **Headings**: Georgia (serif) - Bold weight
- **Body**: Georgia (serif) - Regular weight
- **Characteristic**: Classic serif typography that conveys authority and tradition

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Economist Red | #E3120B | Primary accent, H1 headings |
| Deep Navy | #1F2E7A | Secondary headings, H2 |
| Near Black | #0D0D0D | Body text, H3 headings |
| Mid Gray | #666666 | Captions, secondary text |
| White | #FFFFFF | Background |

### Design Principles
- Clean, uncluttered layouts
- Minimal borders and dividers
- Red used sparingly for maximum impact
- Generous white space
- Traditional editorial feel

## Best Used For
- Research reports
- Market analysis
- Thought leadership pieces
- Editorial content
- Economic analysis
- Policy papers

## Style Specifications
```
H1: Georgia 28pt, #E3120B (Red), Bold
H2: Georgia 18pt, #1F2E7A (Navy), Bold
H3: Georgia 13pt, #0D0D0D (Black), Bold
Body: Georgia 11pt, #0D0D0D
Caption: Georgia 9pt, #666666, Italic
```

## Document Characteristics
- Minimal table borders (0px border-radius)
- Headers-only accent usage
- Conservative, authoritative appearance
- Emphasis on readability and content
